Internship & Research Experience

Embedded Software Development Intern, Dalian Institute of Chemical Physics, Chinese Academy of Sciences

Battery Thermal Runaway Project, Academician Zhongwei Chen's Team   |   May 2026 - Aug. 2026

Worked on an implantable multi-source data acquisition platform for early warning of thermal runaway in new-energy vehicle batteries. The system monitored internal strain, temperature, CO/CO₂/H₂/smoke, PM2.5, and other parameters in real time. I independently designed a high-integration embedded PCB, completed schematic and layout design, and verified signal integrity using oscilloscopes and multimeters. On the software side, I developed a multi-task real-time acquisition system based on STM32F4 and FreeRTOS, including drivers for SPI, IIC, CAN, USART, SD card storage, and multiple sensors. To address the limited space inside batteries, I designed a tab-signal multiplexing scheme that encoded and modulated sensing data onto battery tabs and recovered the information through an external decoding circuit.
